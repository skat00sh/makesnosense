Wait — why π? (the short version)

A needle only crosses a line if it reaches across the gap vertically. Lying flat it reaches almost nothing; standing upright it spans the whole gap. So crossing has nothing to do with circles and everything to do with the needle's tilt.

The catch: to get the overall crossing chance, you have to average over every tilt angle — and the average of that reach, taken across the half-turn from flat to flat, comes out to exactly 2/π. That's the one and only place π enters: it's the size of the angle range you're averaging over. Flip it around and you get the formula in the running calculation above: π ≈ 2N / C.
