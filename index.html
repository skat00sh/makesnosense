The argument that says “always switch”

Say your envelope has A. The other has either A/2 or 2A, each “50/50”. Expected value of switching = 0.5×(A/2) + 0.5×(2A) = 1.25A. That's bigger than A, so switch. But the other envelope's owner can run the exact same argument about your envelope. Both can't be right, and the simulation above shows they aren't: switching wins you nothing on average.
