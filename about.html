About the author

I build the demo I wish I'd had before I believed the math.

My name is Devendra. I make the hard ideas on this site into things you can poke at, because that is the only way I ever actually trusted them myself.

For a long time I learned things the borrowed way: someone hands you an analogy from a field you already know, you nod, and you move on without ever checking whether the analogy carries any weight. It always felt to me a little like paying for the idea on credit. The understanding looks like it is there, until the day you need it and the bill comes due.

So I started rebuilding ideas from their own foundations instead. One small picture instead of a dataset. One running example I could hold in my head. No step I had not done by hand at least once. When I got to the end and the thing still worked, I trusted it, and the trust was the kind you cannot argue me out of, because I had watched it happen.

A good explanation can be argued with. A good demo can't.

That sentence is the whole site. Everything here starts where your intuition is confident but the foundational understanding quietly disagrees, then hands you the controls so you can settle it yourself rather than take my word for it. I would rather you leave disagreeing with me than nodding along, as long as you disagree at the controls.
